CIVIL SERVICE EXAM PRETEST 1

CREATED BY: CSE LEARNING ESSENTIALS

SECTION 2: ANALYTICAL ABILITY

PART 3: LOGICAL REASONING

10-ITEM TEST | PASSING SCORE: 8

Direction: Each item below is given with statements. Identify the best conclusion from the choices that logically follows the given statements.

1.

Statements: All Greeks are philosophers. Socrates is a Greek.
Conclusion:
I. All Greeks are Socrates
II. Socrates is a philosopher.
III. Some Socrates is philosophers.
IV. Some philosophers are Socrates.

2.

Statements: Some actors are singers. All the singers are dancers.
Conclusion:
I. Some actors are dancers.
II. No singer is actor.

3.

Statements: All horses have undivided hoof. All deer have divided hoof.
Conclusion:
I. All deer have undivided hoof.
II. All horses have divided hoof.
III. Some deer have undivided hoof.
IV. Some horses have divided hoof.

4.

Statements: All mammals are animals. All cats are mammals.
Conclusion:
I. All mammals are cats.
II. All cats are animals.
III. Some mammals are cats.
IV. No cats are animals.

5.

Statements: All dogs are mammals. All mammals are animals.
Conclusion:
I. All mammals are dogs.
II. All dogs are animals.
III. Some animals are dogs.
IV. Some animals are mammals.

6.

Statements: All S are M. All M are P.
Therefore:
I. All S are P.
II. Some P are S.
III. No M is S.

7.

Statements: All soldiers are men. Some citizens are soldiers.
Therefore:
I. Some citizens are men.
II. All soldiers are citizens.

8.

Statements: Some ships are boats. All boats are submarines. Some submarines are yachts.
Conclusion:
I. Some yachts are boats.
II. Some submarines are boats.
III. Some submarines are ships.
IV. Some yachts are ships.

9.

Statements: Some mammals are cats. Some cats are white.
Therefore:
I. All cats are mammals.
II. Some mammals are white.

10.

Statements: All dogs are loyal pets. All loyal pets are animals.
Therefore:
I. All dogs are animals.
II. All animals are dogs.
III. Some animals are dogs.

Rationalization

  1. 1 — B
    I. All Greeks are Socrates → False, because Socrates is one Greek, not all Greeks.
    II. Socrates is a philosopher → True, because Socrates is a Greek and all Greeks are philosophers.
    III. Some Socrates is philosophers → Incorrect; “Socrates” is one person, so “some” doesn’t apply.
    IV. Some philosophers are Socrates → Technically true, but syllogisms avoid “some” for an individual. Correct answer: II only.
  2. 2 — A
    I. Some actors are dancers → Valid, since some actors are singers, and all singers are dancers.
    II. No singer is actor → False, because some actors are singers. Correct answer: Only I follows.
  3. 3 — E
    No valid conclusion can follow because we have four different terms (horse, deer, undivided hoof, divided hoof). Correct answer: No valid conclusion.
  4. 4 — B
    I. All mammals are cats → False.
    II. All cats are animals → True, since all cats are mammals and all mammals are animals.
    III. Some mammals are cats → True but optional in strict logic; main valid conclusion is II.
    IV. No cats are animals → False. Correct answer: II only.
  5. 5 — E
    I. All mammals are dogs → False.
    II. All dogs are animals → True.
    III. Some animals are dogs → True, since dogs are animals.
    IV. Some animals are mammals → True, since all mammals are animals. Correct answer: II, III, and IV.
  6. 6 — C
    I. All S are P → True (S → M → P).
    II. Some P are S → True, since all S are P, at least some P are S.
    III. No M is S → False, opposite of the premise. Correct answer: I and II.
  7. 7 — A
    I. Some citizens are men → True, since some citizens are soldiers and all soldiers are men.
    II. All soldiers are citizens → False, only “some” are mentioned. Correct answer: I only.
  8. 8 — B
    I. Some yachts are boats → Not guaranteed.
    II. Some submarines are boats → True, since all boats are submarines.
    III. Some submarines are ships → True, since some ships are boats, and all boats are submarines.
    IV. Some yachts are ships → Not guaranteed. Correct answer: Only II and III follow.
  9. 9 — D
    I. All cats are mammals → False; only some mammals are cats.
    II. Some mammals are white → Not certain, since white cats may or may not belong to “some mammals.” Correct answer: None follows.
  10. 10 — C
    I. All dogs are animals → True, since dogs → loyal pets → animals.
    II. All animals are dogs → False, animals include more than dogs.
    III. Some animals are dogs → True, since all dogs are animals, so at least some animals are dogs. Correct answer: I and III.
